What is this?
An ecosystem where plants live, grow, compete, and evolve on their own.

Every tick, each plant harvests light and water to produce energy. It spends that energy on growing bigger or dropping seeds nearby.

The catch: resources are limited. Tall plants steal light from neighbors. Big roots drain local water. When a plant can't sustain itself, it dies and decomposes into nutrients.

Each seed carries its parent's DNA with tiny random mutations. Over hundreds of ticks, natural selection shapes the population — you're watching evolution happen in real time.

Genome
Each plant has 4 genes (0–1) that
control how it spends energy:

R Roots — water access
H Height — reach light, shade others
L Leaves — more energy, more water use
S Seeds — reproduce vs. grow

R+H+L split the growth budget.
S splits energy between growing and
making seeds. High S = many offspring
but stays small. Low S = grows big
but reproduces slowly.

Seeds inherit genes with small random
tweaks. Over time, different strategies
emerge and compete.
