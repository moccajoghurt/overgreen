What is this?
An ecosystem where plants grow, compete for resources, and evolve. Each seed inherits its parent's DNA with small mutations — natural selection in real time.

Size = leaf area · Darkness = height
Each Tick
1. Water recharge
Each cell regenerates water per tick.
Rate varies by cell (~0.28–0.52).
Max water per cell: 10.
Nutrients decay slowly (−0.02/tick).
2. Light & shadows
All cells start at light = 1.0.
Each taller neighbor (8 surrounding cells)
reduces light by 0.15. Floor: 0.1.
3. Plant update
Water absorption
demand = rootDepth × 0.4
Takes min(demand, cell water).
waterFraction = absorbed / demand
Photosynthesis
energy = light × leafArea × 0.5
  × waterFraction
  × (1 + nutrients × 0.15)
Maintenance cost
0.05 + height×0.05
  + rootDepth×0.03 + leafArea×0.04
Bigger plants cost more to sustain.
Energy budget
energy += produced − maintenance
If energy > 1.0, the surplus is split:
Growth vs. Seeds (gene S)
seedBudget = surplus × S
growthBudget = surplus − seedBudget
Growth allocation (genes R/H/L)
R, H, L are normalized to sum to 1.
Each stat grows by its share × 30%.
Max: height=10, roots=10, leaves=8.
Seed spawning
seeds = ⌊seedBudget / 0.8⌋
Each seed lands randomly within 1–3 cells.
Seedling starts: h/r/l=0.5, energy=2.0.
Genome = parent ± 0.05 per gene.
4. Death
energy ≤ 0 → starvation
age ≥ 500 → old age
5. Decomposition
Dead plant enriches its cell:
+2.0 water, +1.5 + height×0.3 nutrients.
Max nutrients per cell: 10.
Genome
4 genes, each 0–1:
R Root priority — more water access
H Height priority — reach light, cast shade
L Leaf size — more energy, more water use
S Seed investment — reproduce vs. grow

Seeds mutate each gene by ±0.05.
Over generations, strategies diverge.
Initial Conditions
80×80 grid, 40 random plants.
Water per cell: 3–7 (random).
Nutrients per cell: 1–4 (random).
Starting plants: h/r/l=1, energy=3.
Starting genes: random 0.1–0.9.
