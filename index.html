How it works
Each cell has water and nutrients.
Plants absorb water through roots,
harvest light through leaves,
and grow taller to outcompete neighbors.

Tall plants shade shorter ones.
Roots drain local water.
Dead plants decompose and enrich the soil.

Each plant carries a genome that
controls how it allocates energy between
roots, height, leaves, and seeds.
Seeds inherit the parent's genome
with small mutations — evolution emerges.
