# Hook Improvements

Expert review consensus from 6 consultants (game designer, behavioral psychologist, filmmaker, growth PM, science communicator, creative director).

## Session A — Narrative + Camera (next)

### Fix the first frame
The 0s frame is brown mud with invisible seeds and a tiny wordmark in the corner.
- Camera should open much closer — near ground level, single seed centered
- Hold the close-up for 3-4s before beginning the dolly-out
- First sapling visible by second 3-4, not second 8

### Evolution-aware commentary
"Primordial Fern reached 50/300/500 plants" is just a counter incrementing. Trains viewers to think this is a growth sim, not evolution.
- Replace population counters with change statements
- Examples: "First mutation detected", "Two bloodlines diverge", "A new species has emerged"
- The event system already generates these — surface the dramatic ones during hook

### Make speciation the dramatic climax
The first new species should be a visible, named, celebrated event — not silent.
- Large-format announcement when first speciation occurs
- This IS the story. Currently it plays out silently while the commentary counts to 500.

### Scale up "One seed. One world."
Good copy, wasted at 14px in the bottom-left corner where nobody looks.
- Center it, 36-48px, hold as a proper title card for 2-3s
- Fade to lower-left position as camera pulls back

## Session B — Early Interactivity

60 seconds of passive watching before any interactivity is too long.
- Reveal some interactivity by 15-20s (pause button or speed toggle)
- Keep full sidebar hidden longer, but give one button to press

## Session C — Water Edges

The jagged stair-step where water meets terrain screams "unfinished."
- Soften with shoreline color blend, foam ring, or rounded geometry

## Backlog

- **Share affordance** — the 40s frame is the viral moment with no way to capture it
- **Mobile hook** — the concept doesn't translate to 390px screens
- **Typography/UI polish** — real font, consistent color system, proper type scale
