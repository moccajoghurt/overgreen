# Hook Improvements

Expert review consensus from 6 consultants (game designer, behavioral psychologist, filmmaker, growth PM, science communicator, creative director). Ranked by how many independently flagged each issue.

## 1. Fix the first frame (6/6)

The 0s frame is brown mud with invisible seeds and a tiny wordmark in the corner. 20-25% estimated bounce on first paint alone.

- Make the seed an unmissable focal point (lighter soil patch, spotlight vignette, or start mid-growth)
- Camera should open much closer — near ground level, single seed centered
- Hold the close-up for 3-4s before beginning the dolly-out
- First sapling visible by second 3-4, not second 8

## 2. Cut forced wait time (5/6)

60 seconds of passive watching before any interactivity is way too long for a browser experience with no prior reputation.

- Reveal some interactivity by 15-20s (even just a pause button or speed toggle)
- Keep full sidebar hidden longer if desired, but give one button to press
- "The moment someone pushes a button, their session length doubles" — game designer

## 3. Evolution-aware commentary (4/6)

"Primordial Fern reached 50/300/500 plants" is just a counter incrementing. Same species, same verb, escalating number. Trains viewers to think this is a growth sim, not evolution.

- Replace population counters with change statements
- Examples: "First mutation detected", "Two bloodlines diverge", "A new species has emerged"
- The event system already generates these events — surface the dramatic ones during hook, not just post-UI

## 4. Make speciation the dramatic climax (4/6)

The first new species appearing should be a visible, named, celebrated event — not something that happens silently offscreen.

- Full-screen or large-format announcement when first speciation occurs
- Brief color change spreading through part of the forest
- This IS the story. Currently the story plays out silently while the commentary counts to 500.

## 5. Scale up "One seed. One world." (3/6)

Good copy, wasted at 14px in the bottom-left corner where nobody looks.

- Center it, 36-48px, hold as a proper title card for 2-3s
- Fade to lower-left position as camera pulls back
- Consider a timed sequence: "One seed." → "millions of descendants" → "only the fit survive"

## 6. Fix water-terrain staircase edges (2/6)

The jagged stair-step where water meets terrain is the most visible seam in the 3D world. Screams "unfinished."

- Soften with shoreline color blend, foam ring, or rounded geometry
- Single biggest visual quality signal for the 3D aesthetic

## Not yet prioritized

- **Share affordance** (1/6) — the 40s frame is the viral moment with no way to capture it
- **Mobile hook** (1/6) — the concept doesn't translate to 390px screens; may need separate treatment
- **Typography/UI polish** (1/6) — creative director wants a real font (IBM Plex Mono / Space Grotesk), consistent color system, proper type scale
